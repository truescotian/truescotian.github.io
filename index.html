Brain Dump of Lessons Learned

  • Refactoring is important. Even in startups. It saves you from a nightmare later on if you're maintaining it years later.
  • Do the most correct solution, even if it's more work.
  • Have a programmer to look up to and aspire to be as good as them.
  • Hire people, don't contract it out.
  • Spread knowledge between teams, prevent silos.
  • Do not be forced in to a leadership position if you don't want it (Peter Principle).
  • Use typed languages.
  • Version your APIs
  • Do not overcomplicate. Be as simple as possible with everything. Be resistent to breaking changes.
  • E2E tests and integration tests are important.
  • Prevent regressions.
  • In a start-up you'll work too much. It'll be worth it if you succeed.
  • Don't wait for a mentor, they probably wont come.
  • Try not to use inheritance.
  • Don't OOP on frontend.
  • Managers need to make the most use of their best developers. The best developers want / crave a hard problem to solve.
  • If you're unprepared, not engaged, or are not on the ball, people instantly notice.
